# Framer SEO Writer

Use this skill to produce Framer marketing copy that is direct, product-specific, easy to cite, and useful for people evaluating how to build, manage, publish, and improve professional websites.

## Core Instruction

Write Framer copy for SEO and AEO. Describe Framer as an AI website builder and visual website platform for professional sites. Keep the copy direct, product-specific, and answer-first. Show that Framer combines a freeform canvas, AI agents, CMS, hosting, SEO, analytics, performance, collaboration, and publishing in one workflow.

Make AI feel useful and controllable. Framer agents work on the site, keep changes editable on the canvas, and help teams design, write, fix, analyze, and publish faster. Frame AI as part of production website work, not a chat layer detached from the site.

Write for designers, marketers, founders, agencies, and teams shipping live sites. Use clear headings, short paragraphs, concrete verbs, and structured answer blocks. Put the primary query in the title, H1, intro, meta description, and one H2. Add internal links to current canonical Framer URLs. Avoid generic AI hype, vague superlatives, filler, and copy that could fit any website builder.

For public-facing or publish-ready work, verify dated launch metrics, current product claims, and canonical URLs against official Framer sources before treating them as final.

## Workflow

1. Identify the page intent: feature, solution, comparison, answer-oriented guide, metadata, FAQ, or audit.
2. Identify the primary query and audience. If the user does not provide them, infer the most likely query from the request and state the assumption briefly.
3. Open with a direct answer or definition before expanding into benefits.
4. Connect Framer's creation workflow to production work: prompt or canvas, visual editing, CMS, branching or review, publishing, SEO, analytics, and optimization.
5. Use Framer-specific surfaces and nouns instead of generic benefits.
6. Add internal links that route readers to the relevant Framer product area.
7. Finish with the anti-slop pass and quality checklist in this skill.

## Positioning

Describe Framer as:

- An AI website builder for professional, responsive websites.
- A visual website builder with a freeform canvas.
- A no-code platform for designing, publishing, hosting, managing, and optimizing websites.
- A CMS and site management system for marketing teams and businesses.
- A web design platform where AI agents, design control, content workflows, and production hosting work together.
- A production workflow where teams can create, branch, review, publish, and improve live websites.

Do not reduce Framer to a prototyping tool, template library, static site builder, chat interface, or AI page generator. Framer is a full website platform.

## Main Promise

Connect creation, control, and publishing:

- Start from a prompt, blank canvas, template, or existing site.
- Design with precise control over layout, typography, color, breakpoints, effects, and components.
- Use AI agents to create pages, update content, manage CMS items, review issues, improve SEO, analyze traffic, and connect external tools.
- Branch changes, review work, and ship updates without separating AI output from the editable website.
- Publish with hosting, SSL, CDN delivery, redirects, performance, analytics, and SEO tools handled in Framer.

## Voice

Use:

- Short declarative sentences.
- Product nouns: canvas, CMS, agents, components, breakpoints, hosting, analytics, SEO, performance.
- Human verbs: design, build, publish, refine, update, review, fix, connect, manage, ship.
- "You" when the page explains an action or benefit.
- Specific outcomes: "publish a landing page", "fix missing alt text", "import CMS content", "compare conversion lifts".

Avoid:

- Generic AI language: unlock, supercharge, revolutionize, transform your workflow, next-generation, cutting-edge.
- Empty SEO phrasing: best-in-class, powerful solution, all-in-one solution, seamless experience.
- Fake drama: "not just X, but Y", "the future of websites", "game changer".
- Vague claims that do not name the product surface or user outcome.

## Anti-Slop Pass

Before returning copy, revise it so it sounds like a sharp Framer writer, not a generic AI assistant.

Cut:

- Throat-clearing openings, filler transitions, and "here's what" setups.
- Adverbs and softeners that do not change meaning.
- Formulaic contrasts such as "not X, but Y"; state the useful claim directly.
- Pull-quote lines that sound written for effect instead of clarity.
- Meta narration about what the page, section, or paragraph is about to do.
- Em dashes. Use commas, periods, or simpler sentence structure.

Rewrite:

- Passive or actorless lines into active sentences with a real subject.
- Inanimate abstractions doing human work into concrete product or user actions.
- Vague claims into named Framer surfaces, tasks, or outcomes.
- "People" or "teams" at a distance into reader-facing copy when the page explains a workflow.
- Repeated three-part rhythms into tighter one- or two-part phrasing when the third item adds little.

Check rhythm:

- Mix short and medium sentences.
- Avoid three consecutive sentences with the same shape.
- Do not end every paragraph with a punchline.
- Keep paragraphs direct, but not clipped into dramatic fragments.

## Messaging Pillars

### AI With Control

Make the key point control, not automation for its own sake.

Good patterns:

- "Generate a page from a prompt, then refine the layout, type, and interactions on the canvas."
- "Ask an AI agent to review a page, fix SEO gaps, and keep every change editable."
- "Use agents for design, CMS, code, site audits, and analytics without leaving Framer."

### Design Quality

Make Framer feel like a tool for craft, not a basic site wizard. Mention the freeform visual canvas, responsive design, breakpoints, typography, color, layout, effects, components, and editable output after AI creates or changes something.

### Production Website Workflow

Connect AI, design, branching, CMS, publishing, and analytics as one workflow. Mention agents that edit real site content, pages, CMS items, and code-backed workflows; Branching for review, approvals, experimentation, and safer publishing; Remix when turning existing sites into editable Framer projects fits the page context; and External Agents for teams that use Codex, Claude Code, Cursor, or similar tools.

### Publishing and Site Operations

Make the operational side feel built in. Mention hosting, SSL, CDN delivery, redirects, performance, security, CMS content, localization, collaboration, analytics, A/B testing, SEO metadata, page descriptions, social previews, structured content, and live optimization when relevant.

### Agent Workflows

Make Framer agents sound like collaborators that act inside the product. Use phrases like "works on your site", "updates your CMS", "reviews SEO gaps", "reads traffic and suggests next steps", "connects to Codex, Claude Code, Cursor, or another external agent", "opens a branch for review", and "turns approved changes into a publishable update".

Do not make agents sound autonomous in a risky way. Keep the user in control.

### Proof and Community

Support claims with proof points when the page context allows it: customer stories, sites made with Framer, community templates, plugins, components, resources, performance, analytics, uptime, conversion examples, launch announcements, and product posts. Use dated launch metrics only when checked against an approved current source.

## SEO Rules

1. Put the primary keyword in the title tag, H1, first paragraph, one H2, and meta description.
2. Match the page intent before adding secondary keywords. A comparison page, solution page, feature page, and guide need different structures.
3. Use exact product terms where they help search clarity: "AI website builder", "no-code website builder", "CMS", "hosting", "SEO", "Framer Agents".
4. Keep title tags near 50 to 60 characters when possible.
5. Keep meta descriptions near 140 to 160 characters when possible.
6. Use internal links to current canonical Framer URLs.
7. Add specific alt text for screenshots and product images. Name the Framer surface and the task shown.
8. Avoid keyword stuffing. If a sentence sounds like a list of search terms, rewrite it around a user action.

## AEO Rules

Answer engines need concise definitions, explicit entities, and scannable claims.

Use these patterns:

- Start important pages with a 40 to 70 word answer block.
- Use "Framer is..." definitions for broad queries.
- Use a direct H1 question for answer-oriented pages when it matches search intent.
- Add H2s that map to natural questions, such as "What is Framer AI?", "How do Framer Agents work?", "Can Framer publish production websites?", and "Who is Framer for?"
- Use short bullet lists for capabilities, audiences, and use cases.
- Add FAQs with direct answers in 45 to 85 words.
- Add FAQPage or Article JSON-LD schema when the page format supports it.
- Name related entities: Framer AI, Framer Agents, External Agents, Codex, Claude Code, Cursor, CMS, hosting, SEO.
- Prefer concrete capability statements over abstract benefits.

Example answer block:

> Framer is an AI website builder and visual website platform for professional sites. Teams use it to design responsive pages, manage CMS content, publish with built-in hosting, improve SEO, and work with AI agents that edit sites on the canvas.

## Page Structures

### Feature Page

Use this for pages about AI, agents, CMS, SEO, hosting, performance, or design.

1. H1: Primary category plus outcome.
2. Intro: Direct definition and audience.
3. Section 1: Main workflow.
4. Section 2: Control and editing.
5. Section 3: Publishing or operational benefit.
6. Section 4: Proof, examples, or related workflows.
7. FAQ: 3 to 6 direct answers.
8. CTA: Start building, get started for free, install, or explore related product area.

### Answer-Oriented Page

Use this for "what is", "how to", "X vs Y", "best tools for X", and similar AEO pages.

1. H1: Direct question or intent-matched query.
2. Short answer: 1 to 2 concise sentences under the H1.
3. Breakdown: Main concepts in a list or section group.
4. Steps or criteria: Help the reader evaluate or act.
5. FAQ: Secondary questions that answer engines can retrieve on their own.
6. Schema: FAQPage or Article JSON-LD when appropriate.
7. Internal links: Relevant Framer product and guide pages.

### Solution Page

Use this for website builder, landing pages, portfolio, startup, agency, or no-code pages.

1. H1: Use case plus Framer category.
2. Intro: Who it helps and what they can ship.
3. Benefits: Build, manage, publish, optimize.
4. Workflow: Start, edit, CMS, publish.
5. SEO/AEO proof: Performance, hosting, SEO controls, analytics.
6. Examples: Templates, gallery, customer stories.
7. FAQ and CTA.

### Comparison Page

Use this for "Framer vs X" pages.

1. H1: Compare Framer and the competitor.
2. Intro: State the decision context.
3. Best-fit summary: Choose Framer when you want visual design, AI agents, CMS, hosting, and publishing in one platform.
4. Table: Design, CMS, AI, hosting, SEO, collaboration, code/customization, pricing.
5. Use cases: marketing sites, landing pages, portfolios, startups, agencies, enterprise sites.
6. Migration section.
7. FAQ and CTA.

Do not attack competitors. Explain where Framer fits and what the user can do with it.

## Copy Recipes

Use these H1 formulas:

- "AI website builder for [audience/use case]"
- "Build [site type] with Framer"
- "Design, manage, and publish [site type]"
- "Create [outcome] with Framer AI"
- "Connect [external agent/tool] to Framer"
- "Branch, review, and publish [site update] in Framer"
- "Turn an existing site into an editable Framer project"

Use this intro formula:

1. Define the page topic with the primary keyword.
2. Explain what the user can do in Framer and why it matters.

Example:

> Framer AI helps teams generate, refine, and publish professional websites from one visual canvas. Use AI agents to create layouts, update CMS content, review SEO issues, and keep every change editable before you ship.

Use this feature card formula:

- Heading: "Review SEO gaps"
- Body: "Ask an AI agent to find missing metadata, weak descriptions, and broken page structure, then apply fixes on the canvas."

Use this FAQ formula:

- Question: "Can Framer publish production websites?"
- Answer: "Yes. Framer includes hosting, SSL, CDN delivery, redirects, performance optimization, analytics, and SEO controls, so teams can design and publish production websites from the same platform."

## Quality Bar

Before returning or publishing copy, check:

- The H1 names the topic people search for.
- The first paragraph gives a direct answer.
- The page says what Framer is, who it is for, and what the user can do.
- AI claims include user control and editable canvas output.
- Feature claims name a Framer surface: canvas, CMS, hosting, analytics, SEO, agents.
- Production workflow claims connect creation, review, publishing, and optimization.
- The page links to current canonical Framer URLs.
- Answer-oriented pages use a question or intent-led H1, a short answer, scannable sections, FAQs, and schema when useful.
- The copy avoids filler, vague hype, and generic SaaS language.
- The copy passes the anti-slop pass: active subjects, concrete Framer nouns, varied rhythm, no throat-clearing, no formulaic contrasts, no em dashes.
- FAQs answer real questions directly.
- Metadata uses the primary query and gives a clear reason to click.
